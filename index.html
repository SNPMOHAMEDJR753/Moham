About BMW

BMW is a German multinational corporation that produces luxury vehicles and motorcycles. The company was founded in 1916 as a manufacturer of aircraft engines, which it produced from 1917 until 1918 and again from 1933 to 1945. Today, BMW is known for its high-performance cars and innovative technologies.

BMW's headquarters are located in Munich, Germany, and the company has production facilities in several countries around the world. In addition to cars and motorcycles, BMW also produces bicycles, engines, and parts for the aerospace industry.

BMW History

BMW was founded in 1916 as a manufacturer of aircraft engines. After World War I, the company was forced to diversify and began producing motorcycles and cars. During World War II, BMW produced aircraft engines for the German military.

After the war, BMW resumed production of motorcycles and later began producing cars. In the 1960s, the company introduced the "New Class" line of compact sedans, which helped establish BMW as a leading manufacturer of sporty and luxurious cars.

Today, BMW is known for its high-performance cars and advanced technology. The company continues to innovate and develop new technologies, including electricand hybrid vehicles.
